We interviewed teachers! Heres the results:

What are the tech tools Middle School students use most often?

Ms. Viola: Definitely Google Classroom, Gmail, Google Drive, and Canva. Also, how to use a scientific calculator and their personal laptop.

Ms. Mason: I would say students most often use Gmail, Google Classroom, Google Docs, Google Slides, Canva, and IXL.


What is most important to know about each of these tools?

Ms. Viola:

  • Gmail: How to email and CC or BCC people.
  • Google Classroom: Checking to-do lists and knowing how to submit different kinds of assignments.
  • Google Drive: How to make/edit slideshows, sheets, documents, and upload documents.
  • Canva: How to use the free tools to make eye-appealing posters.
  • Scientific Calculator: How to access certain buttons like the square root.
  • Laptop: Download and upload a PDF, connect to Wi-Fi, save passwords.

Ms. Mason:

  • Google Classroom: It’s most important to know how to navigate each class, find assignments, and check due dates.
  • Gmail: Know how to access emails and how often to check them to avoid missing messages.
  • Docs, Slides, and Canva: Learn how to use all the features to make presentations or documents easy to understand and visually appealing.

Where can we access support with these tech tools?

Ms. Viola: Your teachers know a lot about these resources. Mr. Shaker is great at his job also!

Ms. Mason: When I have a question, I try to Google it or watch a YouTube video. If I’m really stuck, I ask Mr. Shaker for help.


What are some important Google Classroom features?

Ms. Viola: The main page that shows deadlines. The Classwork page is super important! Knowing how to upload and submit an assignment.

Mr. Shaker:

  • The ability to track your assignments.
  • The ability to communicate with your teacher.
  • Using it almost like a schedule.

Ms. Mason: The homepage is important because it shows a preview of all your classes with assignments that are due soon. Also, being able to send your teacher a comment on an assignment is an important feature.


What tech advice would you say many middle schoolers could use?

Ms. Viola Email skills are useful and needed. Google Classroom navigation is already pretty good!

Mr. Shaker: Make the most out of the applications you have available. For example, Rocky Hill students have access to great software like Microsoft Office (PowerPoint, Word, etc.) and Adobe products like Photoshop.

Ms. Mason: Many middle schoolers could practice more with sending emails to stay in contact with teachers and communicate any needs.
